# Operation Burning Straits

**Theatre:** Persian Gulf
**Date:** April 1995
**Players:** 2–8

---

**Background**

Following a decade of simmering tensions rooted in the devastation of the 1980–88 Iran-Iraq War, Iran's Revolutionary Guard — hardened by eight years of attritional warfare — has embarked on an aggressive campaign to assert dominance over the Persian Gulf. Citing historical grievances over Western support of Iraq during the war (intelligence sharing, naval escorts, and the silence after chemical attacks on Iranian troops), Tehran's new hardline leadership has declared the Strait of Hormuz a sovereign waterway under Iranian protection.

**The Crisis**

Iranian forces have deployed Silkworm anti-ship missile batteries along the coast, laid mines in commercial shipping lanes, and begun seizing tankers flying Gulf state flags — echoing the "Tanker War" of 1987–88 but on a larger scale. Kilo-class submarines patrol the strait while swarms of IRGC fast attack craft operate out of islands and coastal hideouts. Abu Musa and the Tunb islands have been fortified into forward operating bases bristling with radar and SAM systems.

After an Iranian mine damages a US destroyer near the Strait and IRGC naval forces fire on a coalition helicopter, Washington authorizes a military response.

**Coalition Response**

A US-led coalition — including Saudi Arabia, UAE, and the UK — assembles under Combined Task Force 77. A carrier strike group takes position in the Gulf of Oman while coalition air assets stage out of Al Dhafra, Al Minhad, and naval platforms. The objective: neutralize Iranian anti-access capabilities, reopen the Strait of Hormuz, and degrade IRGC naval and coastal missile infrastructure.

**Situation**

Iran's integrated air defense network — a mix of aging HAWK batteries, domestically upgraded SA-2s and SA-6s, and newer acquisitions — covers the coastline. F-14A Tomcats (remnants from the Shah era, kept flying through reverse-engineered parts) and MiG-29s form the backbone of their air defense. The IRGC operates independently from the regular military, creating an unpredictable dual command structure.

The coalition holds technological superiority but faces a complex littoral environment: dense civilian shipping, oil platforms, confined airspace over the strait, and an adversary experienced in asymmetric tactics refined over years of brutal war.

## Mission Briefs

---

### Mission 1 — "Sledgehammer"

**Type:** SEAD / DEAD
**Time:** 0430L (pre-dawn)
**Situation:** Iranian integrated air defense network must be degraded before strike packages can operate over the Strait. Priority targets are the SA-6 on Abu Musa and the HAWK on Greater Tunb.

| Flight | Aircraft | Role | Tasking |
|---|---|---|---|
| WARHAWKS 1 | F-16C (HTS, 2x HARM) | **Primary SEAD** | Hunt and kill radiating SAM radars. SA-6 SURN and HAWK TR are priority. |
| BLACKSHEEP 1-1 to 1-4 | F/A-18C (JSOW, JDAM) | **DEAD** | Destroy SA-6 launchers and search radar on Abu Musa. Secondary: HAWK launchers on Greater Tunb. |
| PANTHER 1-1 / 1-2 | F-15C (AI) | Escort | Sweep ahead, engage any scrambled fighters. |
| OVERLORD | E-3A | AWACS | 251.0 AM, south orbit |
| SHELL 1 | KC-135 | Tanker | 317.0 AM, TACAN 57X |

**WITCHER — Optional Objective:**
WITCHER 1 (M-2000C, Super 530D, Magic II, Mk-82) sweeps the eastern flank ahead of the package. **Optional:** Destroy the 1L13 EWR on Qeshm Island. Blinding this radar degrades GCI coverage for Iranian fighters, making the MiG-29 scramble response less coordinated. Low-priority — do not pursue if air threats are active.

**OUTLAWS — Optional Objective:**
OUTLAWS 1-1 / 1-2 (AH-64D, Hellfire, rockets) insert low-level from the south using island cover. **Optional:** Destroy ZSU-23-4 AAA positions on Abu Musa's southern shore before BLACKSHEEP's ingress. Success opens a safer attack corridor for the DEAD flight. Coordinate timing — AAA must be down before BLACKSHEEP crosses the beach.

**Threat:** SA-6 engagement range 24km. HAWK at Greater Tunb covers the withdrawal lane. MiG-29A x2 scramble from Bandar Abbas within 8 min of first shots. Dense AAA over Abu Musa below 8,000ft.
**ROE:** Weapons free on radiating SAM systems. PID required on all air contacts. OVERLORD controls.

### Mission 2 — "Iron Fist"

**Type:** Anti-Ship Strike
**Time:** 0530L
**Situation:** IRGC Silkworm and C-802 batteries on Qeshm and Abu Musa threaten all naval traffic. Houdong missile boats are preparing to sortie from Bandar Abbas. Neutralize coastal AShM infrastructure before the fleet moves into the Strait.

| Flight | Aircraft | Role | Tasking |
|---|---|---|---|
| BLACKSHEEP 1-1 to 1-4 | F/A-18C (Harpoon, SLAM-ER, Walleye) | **Primary strike** | Destroy Silkworm battery on Qeshm and C-802 battery on Abu Musa. Standoff engagement. |
| WARHAWKS 1 | F-16C (HTS, HARM) | **SEAD escort** | Suppress any coastal SAMs that survived Mission 1. Protect BLACKSHEEP during their attack run. |
| PANTHER 1-1 / 1-2 | F-15C (AI) | Barrier CAP | South of Strait, block fighters pushing south. |

**WITCHER — Optional Objective:**
WITCHER 1 (M-2000C, Magic II, BLG-66 Belouga) runs armed reconnaissance toward Sirri Island. **Optional:** Locate and destroy the Houdong missile boat anchorage reported near Sirri. Attack with cluster munitions on the pier area. Prevents a second wave of missile boats from threatening the carrier group.

**OUTLAWS — Optional Objective:**
OUTLAWS 1-1 / 1-2 (AH-64D, Hellfire x16 each) operate in the littoral zone between Abu Musa and the UAE coast. **Optional:** Interdict IRGC Boghammar fast attack craft staging from Abu Musa's eastern harbor. Use Longbow radar to distinguish military from civilian traffic. PID mandatory.

**Threat:** Silkworm sites defended by AAA and MANPADS. Expect F-14A intercept from Bandar Abbas. Civilian shipping heavy.
**ROE:** Cleared to engage all designated military targets. Avoid civilian port infrastructure. Positive ID required before Harpoon launch — shipping lanes are active.

### Mission 3 — "Blind Eagle"

**Type:** Defensive Counter-Air / Fleet Defense
**Time:** 0600L (rotating CAP)
**Situation:** Iran is surging fighters to challenge coalition air superiority. IRIAF is launching coordinated strike packages toward the fleet and tanker assets. Maintain air dominance over the Strait.

| Flight | Aircraft | Role | Tasking |
|---|---|---|---|
| BLACKSHEEP 1-1 to 1-4 | F/A-18C (AIM-120C, AIM-9X) | **Primary CAP** | Station over central Strait, FL250. Protect the fleet and support assets. |
| WARHAWKS 1 | F-16C (AIM-120C, AIM-9X, HTS) | **High CAP / Intercept** | Station northeast, FL280. First responder to MiG-29 and F-14A threats. HTS provides SA on surviving SAMs. |
| PANTHER 1-1 / 1-2 | F-15C (AI) | Western CAP | Cover Sirri Island axis against Bushehr-based F-5Es. |
| OVERLORD | E-3A | AWACS | GCI control, assigns targets. |

**WITCHER — Optional Objective:**
WITCHER 1 (M-2000C, Super 530D, Magic II) flies low CAP at FL150, screening the fleet's northern flank. **Optional:** OVERLORD vectors WITCHER to intercept a low-altitude F-5E Tiger II raid inbound from Bushehr. The F-5E is small, fast at low level, and hard to acquire on radar. WITCHER's RDI radar and Magic II are well-suited. Success prevents a pop-up anti-ship attack on coalition vessels.

**OUTLAWS — Optional Objective:**
OUTLAWS 1-1 / 1-2 (AH-64D, Stinger, Hellfire, gun) deploy to FARP "Oasis" on Sir Abu Nu'ayr island. **Optional:** Iranian special operations forces are preparing a beach assault using Zodiacs and a landing craft. OUTLAWS defend the FARP and engage any amphibious approach from the north. Defensive hold — maintain station, report contacts.

**Expected air threats:**
- MiG-29A (R-27R/T, R-73) — most dangerous, 2–4 ship formations
- F-14A (AIM-54A, limited serviceability) — 2-ship, long-range BVR
- F-4E (AIM-7E) — medium altitude, hit-and-run
- F-5E (low altitude raid) — hard to detect, close-in

**ROE:** Weapons free inside declared kill box. PID required outside. OVERLORD controls.

### Mission 4 — "Thunder Run"

**Type:** Anti-Surface Warfare / Littoral Strike
**Time:** 0700L
**Situation:** IRGC Boghammar swarms are dispersing from island harbors across the Strait. Formations of 3–5 boats are threatening coalition warships and attempting to lay additional mines.

| Flight | Aircraft | Role | Tasking |
|---|---|---|---|
| BLACKSHEEP 1-1 to 1-4 | F/A-18C (Mk-20 Rockeye, Mk-82, ATFLIR) | **Primary strike** | Armed recon of the Strait. Locate and destroy Boghammar groups. ATFLIR for PID at range. |
| WARHAWKS 1 | F-16C (AIM-120C, AIM-9X, Mk-82) | **Sweep / light strike** | Sanitize airspace above the littoral zone, then assist BLACKSHEEP on surface targets. |
| PANTHER 1-1 / 1-2 | F-15C (AI) | High cover | Air superiority umbrella. |

**WITCHER — Optional Objective:**
WITCHER 1 (M-2000C, ATLIS pod, GBU-12) operates as forward spotter along the Qeshm coastline. **Optional:** Locate the IRGC command boat directing swarm attacks, believed sheltering near an oil platform complex east of Qeshm. Designate with ATLIS and strike with GBU-12. Destroying the command boat disorganizes remaining Boghammar groups.

**OUTLAWS — Optional Objective:**
OUTLAWS 1-1 / 1-2 (AH-64D, Hellfire, rockets, gun) are the primary anti-surface helicopter asset. **Optional:** Push north along the island chain to the Boghammar staging area at Larak Island. Destroy boats at anchor and any fuel/ammunition depot on the pier. Deep in threat territory — MANPADS expected. Use terrain masking, engage from maximum Hellfire range.

**Threat:** Boghammars carry RPGs, heavy MG, some with C-701 AShM. They use oil platforms and dhow traffic as cover. Below 5,000ft AGL puts aircraft in MANPADS range from islands.
**ROE:** PID mandatory. Heavy civilian small boat traffic. Cleared hot only on confirmed IRGC military vessels.

### Mission 5 — "Persian Storm"

**Type:** Deep Strike (Night)
**Time:** 2100L
**Situation:** IRIAF continues to regenerate sorties from Bandar Abbas. Command has authorized a night strike to permanently degrade Iranian air capability in the theater. Targets: Bandar Abbas airfield infrastructure and the Qeshm radar complex.

| Flight | Aircraft | Role | Tasking |
|---|---|---|---|
| BLACKSHEEP 1-1 to 1-4 | F/A-18C (JDAM, LGB, ATFLIR, NVG) | **Primary strike** | Strike Bandar Abbas airfield: HAS shelters, runway intersection, fuel depot. Precision weapons only. |
| WARHAWKS 1 | F-16C (HTS, HARM, NVG) | **SEAD** | Suppress Bandar Abbas SA-15 and surviving SA-2. Lead the package in. SA-15 is IR-guided — no RWR warning. Use HTS to find the co-located acquisition radar. |
| PANTHER 1-1 / 1-2 | F-15C (AI) | Night sweep | Clear ingress corridor of Shiraz-based CAP. |
| SHELL 1 | KC-135 | Tanker | Post-strike recovery, track EXXON |

**WITCHER — Optional Objective:**
WITCHER 1 (M-2000C, ATLIS pod, GBU-12, Magic II) breaks from the main package 50nm south of Bandar Abbas. **Optional:** Strike the radar complex on eastern Qeshm Island — a Tall King EWR and backup Flat Face search radar. Destroying both creates a permanent gap in Iran's coastal surveillance chain. Night attack with ATLIS is challenging. Approach from the south, use terrain masking from the island ridge. Self-escort with Magic II.

**OUTLAWS — Optional Objective:**
OUTLAWS 1-1 / 1-2 (AH-64D, Hellfire, TADS/PNVS) set an ambush in the narrows between Qeshm and the mainland, NVG flight. **Optional:** Intelligence reports surviving Houdong missile boats will flee Bandar Abbas port southward through the narrows during the air strike confusion. Interdict any naval vessels transiting the narrows during the strike window. Engage with Hellfire at maximum range. Do not proceed north of the narrows — friendly strike traffic overhead.

**Ingress:** Low level through the Strait, pop-up at IP "COBRA" 30nm south of target. Egress southwest over water.
**Threat:** SA-15 at airfield (IR-guided — no RWR warning). SA-2 and dense AAA around the field. Night favors coalition but expect GCI-directed F-4E CAP from Shiraz.
**ROE:** Weapons free on all military targets within the strike box. Avoid civilian terminal at Bandar Abbas International (east side). Precision weapons only near the city.

## Campaign Progression Notes

**Damage carries forward.** If Mission 1 successfully destroys the SA-6, it will not be present in later missions. If the WITCHER destroys the Qeshm EWR in Mission 1, Iranian GCI response in Mission 3 is degraded — MiG-29s launch without coherent vectoring.
